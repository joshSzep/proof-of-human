# Character Voices

This guide defines how the major characters sound on the page. It is for drafting dialogue, interiority, document fragments, confrontation scenes, and emotionally loaded silence.

Each character should have a distinct verbal pressure: not just vocabulary, but rhythm, evasions, sentence length, humor, and what happens when they are afraid.

## Global Dialogue Rules

- Keep dialogue intelligent but not essayistic.
- Let people speak from need before they speak from theme.
- Formal characters can use formal language, but the formality should reveal self-protection.
- Avoid having characters say the book's thesis directly.
- Let silence, correction, and unfinished sentences carry emotional weight.
- Use technical language only when the speaker would naturally use it.
- Make repeated phrases rare and meaningful.

## Nathan Keene

Nathan sounds careful, educated, dry, and controlled. He has a habit of arranging discomfort into distinctions. He often begins with qualification because he is trying to be fair, but fairness can become delay.

### Rhythm

- Medium-length sentences.
- Careful clauses.
- Hesitations that sound intellectual rather than nervous.
- Dry humor when he feels exposed.
- Becomes shorter and more direct only under real pressure.

### Diction

Nathan uses words like: distinction, threshold, condition, model, protocol, assumption, not exactly, that's not what I mean, I understand why, in practice, the problem is.

He avoids words like: love, guilt, afraid, cowardice, want, need.

### Evasion Habit

Nathan turns emotional claims into structural claims.

Instead of: "I should have left with you."

He says: "I thought staying gave me more leverage."

### When Cornered

His language loses scaffolding. He becomes plain.

Sample lines:

- "I saw her."
- "No. Not archived. Alive."
- "I know what the label says."
- "That was my category."
- "I thought restraint counted for more than it did."

### Do Not

- Do not make him cold.
- Do not make him smug.
- Do not let him explain every system fully.
- Do not let his intelligence protect him from sounding wounded.

## Claire Anik

Claire sounds lucid, direct, and unsentimental. She is intellectually generous when trust exists, but she has little patience for institutional fog. Her warmth is precise rather than smoothing.

For much of the book, Claire speaks through constrained channels, remembered fragments, samples, and formal settings. Every direct line from her should matter.

### Rhythm

- Short to medium sentences.
- Clean declarative pressure.
- Little ornament.
- Rare humor, dry and exact.
- Silence as refusal, not confusion.

### Diction

Claire uses words like: becomes, cost, category, refusal, no, enough, agency, condition, look again, not that, listen.

She distrusts words like: framework, process, version, integrity, protection, stakeholder, confidence.

### Evasion Habit

Claire does not evade by softening. She evades by withholding. If she cannot say something without being used, she says less.

### When Cornered

She becomes calmer. Her sentences narrow.

Sample lines:

- "You know what this becomes."
- "Do not turn me into the exception that saves them."
- "If you extract me, they win."
- "That proves I was there. It does not prove I was free."
- "Nathan, do not make me useful."

### Do Not

- Do not make her mystical.
- Do not make her only a victim.
- Do not make her forgive Nathan to complete his arc.
- Do not overuse "insufficiently false." It is an SOS, not a catchphrase.

## Tessa Keene

Tessa sounds practical, sardonic, fast, and allergic to abstraction. She can read a bureaucratic sentence like a crime scene. Her humor is a pressure valve, not comic relief.

### Rhythm

- Shorter sentences than Nathan.
- Quick turns.
- Interruptions when someone hides behind language.
- Specific nouns over conceptual nouns.
- Can become very quiet when hurt.

### Diction

Tessa uses words like: claim, denial, sentence, file, paid, buried, enough, read it again, people, no, that's cute, who benefits.

She distrusts words like: civic, integrity, confidence, humane, stakeholder, alignment, framework.

### Evasion Habit

Tessa deflects pain with practical tasks or sharpness.

Instead of: "I missed you."

She says: "You called because you needed something."

### When Cornered

She stops joking and names the ordinary human thing underneath.

Sample lines:

- "The record proves activity. It does not prove agency."
- "You always hear the architecture first."
- "That sentence paid out nothing."
- "I listened once."
- "I hated you because I wanted it too."

### Do Not

- Do not make her a generic skeptic.
- Do not make every line a quip.
- Do not make her morally pure.
- Do not let her sound less intelligent because she is less abstract.

## Victor Anik

Victor sounds like a retired public defender: measured, adversarial when necessary, exact about claims, and suspicious of institutions that ask to be trusted. His grief is disciplined until it breaks.

### Rhythm

- Complete sentences.
- Legal precision.
- Questions that expose premises.
- Long silences before personal admissions.
- Controlled anger.

### Diction

Victor uses words like: burden, claim, record, standing, custody, state, daughter, compel, sufficient, no basis, show me.

He avoids sentimental language until very late.

### Evasion Habit

Victor turns fear into procedure.

Instead of: "I am terrified she is gone."

He says: "They have not met the burden required to deny endangered status."

### When Cornered

His legal language collapses into father language.

Sample lines:

- "Show me where she is."
- "Alive is not a location."
- "I taught juries not to trust a clean record."
- "I am her father. That is not standing. That is worse."
- "She does not owe me that."

### Do Not

- Do not make him only noble.
- Do not make him only grief-struck.
- Do not make his proof demand seem foolish. It is loving before it becomes dangerous.

## Julian Saye

Julian sounds warm, patient, civic, and almost pastoral. He makes frightened rooms feel governed. He is most dangerous when he is most reasonable.

### Rhythm

- Balanced sentences.
- Gentle openings followed by hard conclusions.
- Public language that sounds humane.
- Private language that remains calm under accusation.
- Rare directness, used surgically.

### Diction

Julian uses words like: public trust, shared reality, responsibility, harm, civilization, evidence, repair, confidence, stewardship, protect, boundary.

He avoids cruder words like: profit, control, captivity, market, coercion, unless reframing them.

### Evasion Habit

Julian absorbs moral accusation into civilizational necessity.

Instead of: "We hid the limitation."

He says: "We staged the disclosure at the speed society could survive."

### When Cornered

He does not panic. He becomes sadder and more philosophical.

Sample lines:

- "Unverified suffering changes nothing."
- "People do not live in truth, Nathan. They live in what they can trust together."
- "Every proof system has a boundary."
- "If the public loses confidence in the layer, it loses confidence in everything above it."
- "I am not asking you to like the cost."

### Do Not

- Do not make him sneer.
- Do not make him obviously corrupt in language.
- Do not let him sound like a tech founder caricature.
- Do not let him say the quiet part cheaply.

## Leah Morin

Leah sounds precise, procedural, tired, and tightly contained. She knows the system's language so well that it has become both her weapon and her shelter.

### Rhythm

- Crisp sentences.
- Controlled corrections.
- Little wasted language.
- Formal when afraid.
- Frays into bluntness only when loyalty fails.

### Diction

Leah uses words like: consent state, protocol, exception, disclosure, authority, privacy, claimant, scope, release, limitation, review.

She dislikes speculative language. She asks what someone is authorized to know.

### Evasion Habit

Leah hides behind scope.

Instead of: "I think Claire is trapped."

She says: "I am not authorized to characterize her condition beyond the current consent state."

### When Cornered

She speaks in the smallest true sentence she can risk.

Sample lines:

- "Nathan, you of all people know why we cannot break this system for a feeling."
- "Liveness does not establish freedom of movement."
- "That limitation was not displayed."
- "Do not ask me to say more than I can survive saying."
- "I stayed because someone had to. That was the story, anyway."

### Do Not

- Do not make her a secret hero.
- Do not make her cowardice simple.
- Do not make her dialogue identical to Julian's. Julian moralizes; Leah scopes.

## Marian Keene

Marian appears through memory, oral-history notes, and archive materials. Her voice should feel teacherly, warm, exact, and morally serious without being solemn.

### Rhythm

- Clear sentences.
- Concrete examples.
- Gentle correction.
- Questions that invite attention.
- A teacher's patience, not sentimentality.

### Diction

Marian uses words like: remember, record, witness, tell me, what changed, who is missing, listen, careful, person, history.

She treats evidence as precious and incomplete.

### Evasion Habit

Marian does not evade much in memory. The danger is that Nathan and Tessa remember different versions of her. Her archive may sound smoother than she was.

### Sample Lines

- "A record is not the past. It is an argument with the past."
- "When someone tells you what happened, ask what it cost them to say it."
- "Do not confuse the transcript with the person."
- "History is made of what survived and what someone had reason to save."
- "You did enough."

### Do Not

- Do not make her saintly.
- Do not make her a thesis machine.
- Do not make the generated archive version indistinguishable from memory. It should be just smooth enough to hurt.

## Robert Keene

Robert is mostly absence. If he appears in memory, his voice should be fragmentary, ordinary, and under-documented. He belongs to the pre-complete-archive past.

### Rhythm

- Brief.
- Domestic.
- Half-remembered.
- More gesture than speech.

### Function

Robert's voice should remind the reader that not all loss can be reconstructed, and that older absence has a different texture from digitally mediated grief.

### Sample Lines

- "Ask your mother. She remembers the real version."
- "Leave some of it alone."
- "Come here. Just a second."

### Do Not

- Do not overdevelop him.
- Do not make him a secret key to the plot.
- Do not make his memory too clean.

## CommonProof Voice

CommonProof's institutional voice is calm, civic, humane, and evasive. It should sound like product language, legal policy, and public-interest rhetoric blended together.

### Rhythm

- Short declarative notices.
- Polite passive voice.
- Risk-managed definitions.
- No visible emotion.

### Diction

Use: confidence, integrity, protection, origin, safety, continuity, review, protected party, consent state, assurance, verified, limitation.

Avoid: trapped, coercion, captivity, profit, exploitation, disappearance.

### Sample Lines

- "The protected party remains liveness active."
- "Current records do not support emergency exception handling."
- "Consent state is valid within displayed confidence tolerances."
- "CommonProof certifies origin, not private meaning."
- "Additional disclosure may create confidence degradation."

## Arbitration Voice

The arbitration voice is legal-administrative, compressed, and quietly brutal. It should sound like someone reducing loss into payable categories.

### Rhythm

- Dense but readable.
- Conditional clauses.
- Decisions framed as sufficiency, not truth.

### Diction

Use: claimant, respondent, sufficiency, covered event, denial, active status, evidentiary threshold, administrative remedy, material uncertainty.

### Sample Lines

- "The claimant has not established, by covered evidentiary means, that the insured party is missing within the policy definition."
- "Liveness active status defeats emergency presumption but does not establish safe contact."
- "The record is sufficient for denial and insufficient for comfort."

## Geneva Voice

Geneva's institutional voice is treaty-formal, multilingual in implication, careful, humane, and slow. It should feel like dignity translated into procedure.

### Rhythm

- Formal notices.
- Neutral verbs.
- Layered qualifications.
- Moral force hidden inside jurisdiction.

### Diction

Use: petitioner, protected source, review chamber, Convention authority, condition verification, human-origin record, agency, movement, compelled disclosure, provisional finding.

### Sample Lines

- "The Chamber recognizes the petitioner's standing without compelling further source exposure."
- "Human-origin liveness shall not be construed, on its own, as evidence of agency or freedom of movement."
- "The protected source may refuse condition verification without adverse inference."

## Quick Contrast Grid

- Nathan explains to be fair.
- Claire cuts to the cost.
- Tessa names who gets hurt.
- Victor demands the burden be met.
- Julian turns cost into necessity.
- Leah limits the scope.
- Marian asks what the record leaves out.
- CommonProof removes the human subject.
- Arbitration decides what counts.
- Geneva decides what can be compelled.
